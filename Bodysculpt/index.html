Common questions

Everything you need to know
before you scan.

First time with a 3D body scanner? Here's what clients ask most before booking.

What should I wear for the scan?
Form-fitting shorts and a sports top, or compression wear. The infrared sensors need a clear view of your body shape — loose clothing creates shadow gaps and reduces the accuracy of your measurements. We can provide modesty wraps if preferred.
How long does the whole appointment take?
The scan itself is 35 seconds. A scan-only appointment takes around 15–20 minutes including setup and receiving your emailed report. If you add a consultation, allow 45–60 minutes total.
Does the Styku scanner use radiation or emit anything harmful?
No. Styku uses infrared light — the same technology found in depth cameras and motion capture systems. It is completely safe, non-invasive, and you won't feel anything during the scan. There is no ionising radiation of any kind.
How is this more accurate than a BIA machine or InBody scale?
BIA (Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis) passes a small electrical current through your body and estimates fat from resistance. Results shift significantly based on hydration levels, recent eating, temperature, and time of day — so the same person can get meaningfully different readings hours apart. Styku uses infrared optical measurement validated against a DEXA reference database. That reading doesn't fluctuate with hydration. The two methods are not comparable for accuracy.
What's actually included in the PDF report?
Your report includes: a 3D rotating body avatar viewable from all angles, body fat percentage, lean muscle mass, 400+ circumference measurements (waist, hips, chest, arms, thighs, calves and more), and a full body composition breakdown. If you rescan, the report also generates a side-by-side before and after comparison of every metric.
How often should I rescan to track progress?
Every 4–8 weeks. That's enough time for meaningful changes in body composition to register clearly in the data. Rescanning sooner than 3 weeks risks the differences falling within normal measurement variance rather than reflecting genuine change.
Can I scan if I'm pregnant or have a medical condition?
We recommend waiting until after pregnancy. If you have implanted medical devices or specific health considerations, please check with your doctor before booking. For everyone else, the scan is safe, non-invasive, and completely painless.
Where are you located, and do you come to hotels?
We're based on Koh Samui and work directly with hotels and private villas. We're registered contractors at Anantara Lawana and Meliá Koh Samui, and can arrange on-site scanning at your property. Message us on WhatsApp with your location and we'll work out the details.
